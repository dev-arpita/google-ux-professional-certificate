# Start the UX Design Process: Empathize, Define, and Ideate
In the second course of this UX certificate program, you’ll complete the first phases of the design process for a project that you’ll be able to include in your portfolio. You will learn how to empathize with users, define their needs, and come up with a massive range of solutions.

## The Three Pillars
### 1. Empathize (The "Who")
You'll learn that you are not the user. By conducting research and interviews, you'll uncover the pain points (frustrations) that real people face.

Key Tools: Empathy Maps, Personas, and User Journey Maps.

#### Activity: Create personas for your portfolio project

### 2. Define (The "What")
Once you have your research, you have to make sense of it. You’ll learn to write Problem Statements—clear, concise descriptions of the user’s needs that must be addressed.

Key Tools: Problem Statements and User Stories.

### 3. Ideate (The "How")
This is the creative explosion. Instead of sticking with your first idea, Google teaches you techniques to generate dozens of solutions quickly to find the most innovative one.

Key Tools: Competitive Audits and "Crazy 8s" sketching.

### Empathize with users
Empathy is the ability to understand someone ​else's feelings or thoughts in a situation. 
UX design is not about solving ​problems we assume users want solved. 
​It's about solving problems that ​users actually want solved. ​When we first meet someone, ​we may have a lot of assumptions about who they are. ​As we get to know them though, we may discover that ​our assumptions were not so accurate. ​This is true in UX design, too. ​We might think we know who a user is and what they need, ​but UX research reveals who they really are and what they actually need, ​allowing us to better empathize with the user.

#### Prepare for user interviews
preparing for user interviews in UX design.
**Preparation Steps:**
Script your interview questions to maintain consistency across interviews.
Gather necessary supplies, including printed questions and recording equipment, and familiarize yourself with any technology you'll use.
**Research and Practice:**
Research users by noting their personal information and relevant experience with the product.
Practice delivering your questions to ensure a smooth interview process.
By following these steps, you'll be well-prepared to conduct effective user interviews.

the four essential steps for conducting user interviews in the UX design process.

Meeting the Participant

Establish a friendly rapport to make participants feel comfortable, starting with light conversation and expressing gratitude for their time.
Review any legal details and gather basic demographic information to set the stage for the interview.
Conducting the Interview

Maintain professional etiquette by speaking clearly and asking open-ended questions to encourage detailed responses.
Use follow-up questions to delve deeper into participants' thoughts and feelings about the product.
Taking Notes

Document key quotes and observations during the interview to capture participants' insights and behaviors.
Consider recording the interview (with permission) for later reference and to ensure accuracy in capturing information.
Wrapping Up the Interview

Conclude the interview by inviting participants to share any final thoughts and expressing appreciation for their contributions.
Ensure participants leave feeling valued and informed about any incentives offered for their participation.
