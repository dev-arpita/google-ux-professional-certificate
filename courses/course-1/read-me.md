# Course takeaways:

## The Strategy (Thinking Like a Designer)

- **User-Centered Design (UCD):** A framework where you put the user’s needs at the center of every decision.

- **Design Thinking:** A 5-step process (Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test) used to solve complex problems.

- **Sprint Planning:** Learning how to participate in "Design Sprints", a five-day process for answering critical business questions through design.

## The Human Factor (Understanding the User)

- **User Experience (UX):** The overall "vibe" and ease of use a person has with a product.

- **Persona:** Creating a fictional character (based on research) that represents your target user to keep your goals focused.

- **Usability:** Learning how to measure if a design is actually functional and easy to navigate.

## The Visuals (Building the Product)
- **User Interface (UI):** The specific assets users interact with (buttons, icons, spacing).

- **Wireframing:** Creating low-fidelity "blueprints" of a screen.

- **Mockups:** Static, high-fidelity images of what the final product will look like.

- **Prototyping:** Creating a clickable, interactive version of your design to show how it flows.

## The Core Objectives

### A. Your Role in the Team
You’ll learn that an entry-level UX designer wears many hats. You aren't just "making things pretty." You’ll explore how you collaborate with:

- **Product Managers:** Who define the business goals.

- **Engineers/Developers:** Who actually build the code.

- **UX Researchers:** Who gather the data you use to design.

### B. The "Golden Rules" of Google’s Philosophy

- Google places a massive emphasis on three specific concepts in this course:

- **User-Centered Design:** If it doesn't work for the user, it doesn't work.

**Accessibility:** Designing for people with disabilities (vision, hearing, motor, etc.).

**Equity-Focused Design:** Designing for groups that have been historically underrepresented or ignored.

### C. The Magic of Design Sprints
A Design Sprint is like a **"greatest hits"** of the design process compressed into one week. You’ll learn why companies like Google use them to save time and money by testing ideas before building them.

### The "Pro" Takeaway
The most important thing you’ll learn in Course 1 is that you are not the user. Designers often fall into the trap of designing for themselves. This course trains you to step outside your own head and design for someone else's reality.
